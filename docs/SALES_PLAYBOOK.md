## Sales Call Script

### Opening (30 seconds)

**Goal:** Build rapport and establish authority.

> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with TCC Solutions. I help restaurants modernize their menus with smart QR codes. I'm not going to take much of your time — I just want to show you something that's helping restaurants like yours save time and make more money. Do you have 2 minutes?"

**If they say they're busy:**
> "Totally understand. When would be a better time for a quick 5-minute call? I promise it'll be worth it."

### Discovery (2-3 minutes)

**Goal:** Understand their pain points.

**Questions to ask:**

1. "How do you currently share your menu with customers?"
2. "How often do you update your prices or menu items?"
3. "Do you ever get frustrated answering the same questions over and over — like 'Is this gluten-free?' or 'What do you recommend?'"
4. "Have you thought about letting customers order from their phones?"

**Listen for these pain points:**
- Reprinting menus is expensive and time-consuming
- Staff wastes time answering repetitive questions
- Order mistakes from miscommunication
- No visibility into what's actually selling
- Want to modernize but don't know how

### Presentation (3-5 minutes)

**Goal:** Show the solution matches their needs.

> "Based on what you told me, I think TCC Menus would be perfect for you. Let me explain what it does..."

**Key talking points (use the ones that match their pain points):**

**For "reprinting is expensive":**
> "With TCC Menus, you never print another menu again. You update everything from your phone or computer, and customers see the changes instantly."

**For "staff answering questions":**
> "We have an AI assistant built into the menu. Customers can ask 'Is the pasta gluten-free?' and it answers based on YOUR actual ingredients — not random internet info. Your staff can focus on service instead of playing 20 questions."

**For "order mistakes":**
> "When customers order through TCC Menus, the order goes straight to your kitchen screen. No handwriting to misread. No 'he said, she said.' Just clear orders, every time."

**For "want to modernize":**
> "The best part? Your customers don't download anything. They just scan a QR code with their regular phone camera and they're in. Works on any phone, any browser."

### Demo Offer

> "I'd love to show you exactly how it works. If you pull up tccmenus.com/demo on your phone right now, you can scan our demo menu and see it in action."

*Walk them through the demo if they're willing*

### Pricing Presentation

**Always present Professional first — it's the best value.**

> "Most restaurants go with our Professional plan. It's $499 to get set up, and then $99 a month after that. That includes everything — unlimited menu items, the AI assistant, online ordering, kitchen display, analytics, the works."

**If they push back on price, go to Starter:**
> "If you want to start smaller, we have a Starter plan at $299 setup and $49/month. It's great for getting your feet wet with the QR menu before adding ordering."

**If they're a bigger operation:**
> "For multi-location restaurants, we have Enterprise at $799 setup and $149/month. That includes POS integration with Clover or Square, plus a dedicated account manager."

### Close

**Trial close:**
> "Based on everything we talked about, which plan sounds like the best fit for your restaurant?"

**Assumptive close:**
> "Let's get you set up. I can have your menu live by tomorrow. What's the best email to send the signup link to?"

**Urgency close (use sparingly):**
> "We're running a promotion this month where we waive the first month's subscription if you sign up this week. Want me to lock that in for you?"

## Handling Objections

### "It's too expensive"

**Response:**
> "I totally get it — every dollar matters in this business. Let me ask you this: how much do you spend reprinting menus every year? And how many hours does your staff spend answering questions that could be automated? Most of our restaurants save the monthly cost in labor alone within the first few weeks."

**Alternative:**
> "What if we started with the Starter plan at $299? That gets you the digital menu without ordering, so you can see the value before upgrading."

### "We already have a POS system"

**Response:**
> "Perfect — TCC Menus doesn't replace your POS. It works alongside it. Think of it as adding a layer for your customers, not replacing anything you already have. And if you ever want to connect them, we integrate with Clover and Square."

### "Our customers are older / not tech-savvy"

**Response:**
> "That's actually one of our biggest advantages. There's no app to download — customers just point their phone camera at a QR code and the menu opens. If they can take a photo, they can use TCC Menus. And for customers who prefer paper, you can still have paper menus — this is just an option."

### "We're too busy right now"

**Response:**
> "I hear that a lot — restaurants are always busy! That's actually why this is perfect timing. We do all the heavy lifting. Just send us your menu and we set everything up. You'll spend maybe 30 minutes total, and then you start saving time every single day."

### "I need to think about it"

**Response:**
> "Absolutely, take your time. Before I let you go, what's the main thing you're unsure about? I want to make sure I've answered all your questions."

*Listen, then address their specific concern*

**If still hesitant:**
> "How about this — I'll send you a link to our demo. Play with it when you have a few minutes, show your staff, and let's reconnect in a day or two. When would be a good time to follow up?"

### "I've tried digital menus before and they didn't work"

**Response:**
> "I appreciate you sharing that. Can I ask what went wrong? ... That makes sense. The difference with TCC Menus is [address their specific issue]. We've designed this to be simple for both you and your customers. That's why we offer a 30-day money-back guarantee — if it doesn't work for you, you're not stuck."

### "Can I get a discount?"

**Response:**
> "I wish I could, but our pricing is already pretty competitive for everything you're getting. What I CAN do is make sure we get your menu looking perfect and include some extra support in your first month. Does that work for you?"

**If they really push (and it's a good prospect):**
> "Tell you what — I can waive the first month's subscription fee. That saves you $99 right there. But I can only do that if we get started today."

*Note: Never go below plan pricing. The discount is on the first month only.*

### "Let me talk to my partner/business partner"

**Response:**
> "Of course! Is there anything specific you think they'd want to know? I can send over some info that addresses their concerns... Also, would it help if we did a quick call together? I'm happy to present to both of you."

### "We don't need online ordering"

**Response:**
> "No problem at all — online ordering is optional. You can use TCC Menus as a view-only digital menu. Customers scan, browse, and see your beautiful menu, but ordering still goes through your staff. You can always turn on ordering later when you're ready."

## Sales Rules

### Pricing Rules

1. **Maximum setup fee: $800** — No exceptions. This means Enterprise ($799) is the ceiling.
2. **Never discount the setup fee** — The prices are the prices.
3. **You may waive the first month's subscription** — Only as a closing incentive for hesitant prospects.
4. **No free trials** — We have a 30-day money-back guarantee instead.

### Commission Structure

- **Commission rate: 90% of the setup fee**
- Starter ($299 setup) = $269.10 commission
- Professional ($499 setup) = $449.10 commission
- Enterprise ($799 setup) = $719.10 commission

### Conduct Rules

1. **Never badmouth competitors** — Focus on our strengths, not their weaknesses.
2. **Never promise features we don't have** — If unsure, say "Let me check on that and get back to you."
3. **Always be honest** — If TCC Menus isn't right for them, say so. Our reputation matters.
4. **Follow up promptly** — Respond to leads within 24 hours maximum.
5. **Log every interaction** — Keep notes on every call and meeting.

### Qualification Checklist

Before spending too much time on a prospect, confirm:

- [ ] They have a restaurant, cafe, food truck, or similar food service business
- [ ] They have at least some customers who use smartphones
- [ ] They have decision-making authority OR can connect you to someone who does
- [ ] They're not under contract with a competitor for another 6+ months
- [ ] They seem interested, not just "browsing"

## Ideal Customer Profile

**Best prospects:**
- Full-service restaurants (casual to upscale)
- Fast-casual restaurants

- Bars with food menus

**Warning signs (harder to close):**

- Extremely small operations (can't afford it)
- Recently signed with a competitor
- No smartphone usage among their customers

## Common Questions & Answers

**Q: How long does setup take?**
A: Most restaurants are live within 24 hours. Just send us your menu and we handle everything.

**Q: Do customers need to download an app?**
A: No! That's the beauty of it. They just scan a QR code with their regular camera.

**Q: What if my prices change?**
A: You update them from your phone or computer in seconds. Changes appear instantly.

**Q: Can we try it first?**
A: We have a 30-day money-back guarantee. If you're not happy, we refund everything.

**Q: Does this replace our POS?**
A: No, it works alongside your existing systems. Integration with Clover/Square is optional.

**Q: What happens if the internet goes down?**
A: Customers can still access the menu on their own data. The QR code works as long as they have any internet connection.

**Q: Are there any hidden fees?**
A: No. Setup fee, monthly subscription, and that's it. Payment processing is standard Stripe rates (about 2.9% + 30¢).
